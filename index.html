About KASCON

The Korean American Students Conference (KASCON) is the largest and oldest student-run ethnic-specific conference in the United States. Since its inception at Princeton University in 1987, KASCON has touched the lives of thousands of Korean Americans, bringing strength and cohesion to the greater Korean American community by showcasing successful Korean American leaders from a variety of industries and fields, including business, politics, and fashion. Through various workshops, speaker panels, and networking events, KASCON aims to facilitate conversations and discourse about the ways in which our bicultural values shape our personal and professional developments, making positive differences in our respective communities and beyond.

The Mirae Foundation

Founded in 1991, the Mirae Foundation is an independent, 501(c)3 not-for-profit organization that devotes 100% of its resources to endeavors such as KASCON in order to foster leadership in new generations of the Korean American community. The Foundation serves as a permanent advisory body to KASCON, linking past and current organizers, speakers, and supporters.

2018 Conference

The theme of this year’s conference will be Dimensions, which aims to spotlight the multiple facets that make up our Korean American identities. Dimensions describes our combined struggles to find balance between our “Koreanness” and our “Americanness,” showing how two irreconcilable extremes are actually more integrated than we realize. Acknowledging that our identities are neither singularly Korean nor American is central to the vision of KASCON 29: Dimensions.

FAQ

Who can attend?

KASCON 29 is open to students of all ethnicities and backgrounds. As long as you are interested in Korean American issues and experiences, you are more than welcome to attend.

What is the cost to attend?

We offer early bird, regular, and late registration tickets, all at varying prices. Please look at REGISTRATION for more information.

Can we register in groups?

We are currently working on making this available for student organizations that are interested in coming as groups. Please email us at kascon29@gmail.com to discuss further options. More information will be posted soon.

What will the schedule of the conference look like?

The conference will take place from March 16 to 18, 2018. Over the course of three days, there will be an assortment of panels, seminars, and activities featuring speakers from a variety of industries and fields, including politics, fashion, and journalism. We will also conclude the conference with a concert and after party. Please check back soon for a more detailed schedule, an updated list of our speakers, and our performers!

Will there be travel accomodations?

There are no travel accommodations provided by KASCON29, and all of the hotels listed above are within walking distance of Northwestern's campus. If you plan on staying outside Evanston, we urge you to take advantage of Chicago's public transportation. The Purple Line has 3 stops in Evanston, and the Metra trains have a couple of stops in Evanston as well. Both trains either go directly to downtown Chicago or require a transfer. The Purple Line map and schedule can be accessed here and the Metra map can be found here.
