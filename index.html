About KASCON

Since its birth in 1987 at Princeton University, the Korean-American Students Conference (KASCON) has touched the lives of thousands of Korean-American students. The Conference has helped them explore their heritage and shape their future roles in American society by bringing them in touch with each other and with prominent Korean-American role models. Now in its third decade of existence, KASCON enjoys a success unimagined by its original founders and past organizers.

About the Mirae Foundation

Past organizers created the Mirae Foundation to link Conferences to one another and to ensure that KASCON remains a student-run conference that also benefits from the advice and experience of past organizers, speakers, and supporters. The Foundation consists of the Board of Directors, which provides strategic vision to the Foundation, and the Executive Team, which liases with the Conference board and manages the day-to-day operations of the Foundation.

2018 Conference

KASCON 29: Dimensions aims to elevate the conversation around Korean-American identity to be more encompassing of the variety of experiences that constitute the Korean-American experience. The struggle to find a sense of identity is not one that is unique to the Korean-American community, but the ways in which that struggle manifests are what makes our community unique. Trying to find a balance between our "Koreanness" and our "Americanness" makes the Korean-American experience one that seems like a spectrum of two extremes when the reality is, our experiences are more nuanced and multi-dimensional. Acknowledging that our identities are neither singularly Korean nor American nor are they just Korean-American is central to the vision of KASCON 29: Dimensions.

While the conference is geared toward Korean-American undergraduate students, anyone is welcome to attend. You do not need to be of Korean heritage, but an interest in Korean-American issues and experiences is required!
